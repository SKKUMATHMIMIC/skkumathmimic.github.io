Drawing strong interest with around 50 applicants, the competition unfolded in three stages—a preliminary exam, a Golden Bell round, and a main tournament—and was designed to demand not just speed in reaching an answer, but a deep understanding of integration paired with quick thinking. Based on the preliminary results, twelve top-scoring participants were selected: the top four advanced directly to the main tournament, while those who followed competed for the remaining spots in the Golden Bell round, where each participant, whiteboard in hand, took on one survival-or-elimination problem after another. Through the Golden Bell round, a total of eight participants reached the main tournament and faced off starting from the quarterfinals. Across the quarterfinals and the semifinals, those who completed accurate solutions within the time limit advanced one step at a time, and whenever a tie occurred, overtime and lightning rounds produced tense moments in which a single problem decided the outcome. The two participants who came through the semifinals contended for the championship in a five-problem final, while those who fell short in the semifinals competed for the standings in a third-place match. The winning participants received prizes including AirPods, a JBL Bluetooth speaker, and a Stanley tumbler.
