The team was formed by club members who wanted to explore applied mathematics, and consisted of Kwon-Woo Yang, Eun-Seo Jeong, Su-Yeon Cho, Chae-Young Han, and Sun-Woo Hong, under the advisement of Professor Yun-Mo Jeong of the Department of Mathematics, Sungkyunkwan University. Their winning project, titled "Data-Adaptive Training-Interval Control for Diffusion Models: A New Noise-Learning Strategy to Improve Generative Performance," proposed a training strategy that adjusts the noise-learning interval of a diffusion model according to the characteristics of the training data. The team implemented the strategy directly and trained a diffusion model that actually generated images, using the results to demonstrate an improvement in generative performance over the baseline approach.
