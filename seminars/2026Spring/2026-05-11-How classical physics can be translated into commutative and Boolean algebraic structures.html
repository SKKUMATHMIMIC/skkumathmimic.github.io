The state space in Hamiltonian mechanics can be interpreted as a commutative algebra of physical quantities, and a state can in turn be understood as a morphism on that algebra assigning values to those quantities. Furthermore, imposing a condition on a physical quantity gives rise to an event, and the collection of such events forms a Boolean algebraic structure that handles truth and falsity.
