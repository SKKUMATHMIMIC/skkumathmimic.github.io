A regression model is a core language of statistics that quantitatively describes the relationships between variables, predicts unobserved values, and accounts for the uncertainty inherent in data. In this seminar, we will first organize the basic concepts of regression, centered on simple and multiple linear regression, including the interpretation of regression coefficients, the meaning of error, and the measurement of explanatory power. We will then introduce Ridge regression, which addresses multicollinearity, LASSO regression, which performs variable selection, and logistic regression, which handles categorical response variables, examining how classical linear regression extends into broader statistical models. After understanding why this extension is justified through decision theory, we will finally look at how regression analysis functions across today's data analysis landscape through its connections to time-series data and machine learning. Through this, we hope to view regression not merely as a computational technique, but as a structure of statistical thinking for understanding data and inferring the future. prerequisite_label_en: "Prerequisite" prerequisite_en: "Linear Algebra (or basic knowledge of matrices), Probability and Statistics or Introduction to Statistics" # Media and materials (shared) video_heading: "Video Link" video_url: "https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bkJayfrNkJM" thumbnail_src: "/seminars/2026Spring/thumbnails/9_regression.jpg" thumbnail_alt: "Regression and Its Applications thumbnail" materials_heading: "Presentation Material" materials: - label: "Slide deck: Regression and Its Applications" url: "/seminars/2026Spring/slidedecks/9_regression.pdf" ---
