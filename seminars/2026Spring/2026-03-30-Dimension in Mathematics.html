We have come to understand dimension as the minimum number of coordinates needed to specify the position of a point in space, or as the degrees of freedom along which a point can move. Just as a line differs from a plane, dimension serves as a powerful indicator that distinguishes the essential differences between objects. To treat this property rigorously, mathematicians have redefined dimension in ways suited to each branch of mathematics. In this seminar, we will look at the various notions of dimension defined across fields such as linear algebra, topology, and fractal geometry. In particular, we will introduce intriguing cases in which our long-held intuition of dimension as "the minimum number of variables needed to specify a position" breaks down, and examine how mathematicians have tried to redefine "dimension" to overcome these cases. Through this process, we hope to think together about what dimension truly means in mathematics. prerequisite_label_en: "Prerequisite" prerequisite_en: "Linear Algebra, Topology" # Media and materials (shared) video_heading: "Video Link" video_url: "https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YMQD0uYoNLo" thumbnail_src: "/seminars/2026Spring/thumbnails/7_dimension.jpg" thumbnail_alt: "Dimension in Mathematics thumbnail" materials_heading: "Presentation Material" materials: - label: "Slide deck: Dimension in Mathematics" url: "/seminars/2026Spring/slidedecks/7_dimension.pdf" ---
